# Cogence Principles

## 1. Business First

Managers care about outcomes, risks, priorities, and progress.

Engineering details should be translated into business language whenever possible.

## 2. Signals Over Surveillance

Cogence exists to provide visibility, not employee monitoring.

The platform should avoid ranking individuals by simplistic metrics such as commit counts, lines of code, or activity volume.

The goal is organizational understanding, not workforce surveillance.

## 3. Context Before Metrics

Metrics without context create misleading conclusions.

Every insight should include enough context to support informed decision-making.

## 4. Explainability Over Mystery

Users should understand why a conclusion was produced.

Cogence should prefer transparent reasoning over opaque scoring systems.

## 5. Incremental Intelligence

Start with simple observations.

Add sophistication only when it produces measurable value.

Avoid complexity for its own sake.

## 6. Human-Centered Reporting

Reports should be readable in minutes, not hours.

The best report is one that is actually consumed and acted upon.

## 7. Trust Is Mandatory

Insights must be reliable, traceable, and grounded in evidence.

When confidence is low, uncertainty should be communicated explicitly.

## 8. Actionability Over Information

The purpose of a report is not to describe reality.

The purpose of a report is to help someone make a better decision.

Every insight should answer:

"So what?"
